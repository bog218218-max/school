# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary users are students in grades 9 to 11 preparing for EGE or OGE. They arrive with anxiety, scattered materials, unclear priorities, and a need to understand what to do today. Parents are the influencing audience: they need evidence that preparation is structured, visible, calm, and reliable.

Teachers and administrators are not the main landing page audience, but the product should imply a real operating system behind the scenes: mentors, feedback, assignment checks, mock exams, and route updates.

## Product Purpose

Marshrut EGE turns exam preparation from a chaotic set of lessons and materials into a managed personal route. The product starts with diagnostics, builds a plan, gives practice around weak topics, tracks progress, involves a mentor, and reduces overload.

The main conversion action is to pass diagnostics and receive a preliminary preparation route. The site should show a complete product path: visitor understands the problem, sees the route system, passes diagnostics, receives a route result, opens a demo dashboard, and can leave a request.

## Brand Personality

Calm, technological, precise, supportive. The brand should feel like a navigator that leads the student through a clear plan without fear-based pressure.

The tone is premium and calm with a modern youth-facing edge. It should be understandable to parents while staying attractive to students.

## Anti-references

Do not use aggressive exam marketing, panic, red CTAs, fear of failure, meme-heavy student style, childish visuals, black backgrounds, acid colors, hard corners, dense legacy tables, or claims such as "100 points in a month" and "guaranteed result".

Avoid generic education copy such as "quality education" and "best teachers". The site should prove value through mechanics: diagnostics, route, practice, mock exams, progress, weak topics, mentor, and parent reports.

## Design Principles

Show the system, not promises. The interface should repeatedly make the route visible through dashboards, progress, weak topics, next steps, and plans.

Calm before urgency. Conversion should come from clarity and control, not fear.

Personalization should be concrete. Every claim about personalization should map to a selected goal, level, subject, weak topic, or recommended next step.

Parents need transparency without pressure. Parent-facing content should explain progress, reports, mock exam dynamics, and mentor communication in plain language.

Prototype like a product. The site should include interactive diagnostics, a result screen, and a demo personal dashboard rather than only static marketing sections.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, clear focus states, keyboard-accessible forms, visible labels, reduced-motion support, and mobile-first layouts. Use calm motion, no flashing effects, and no fear-based language.
